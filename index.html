Portfolio story

Assess the environment, identify risk, recommend controls, and formalize the response.

These academic artifacts were designed as a connected sequence. The first case study identifies information-security weaknesses and recommends technical, administrative, and physical controls. The second translates those recommendations into a structured security plan. The third applies a policy-review lens to acceptable use, authentication, incident response, and third-party access expectations.

The work is presented as coursework and uses a fictional scenario. It does not represent employer work, legal advice, or a production security assessment.

Control themes

Security and governance concepts applied

Identity & Access

Named accounts, least privilege, MFA, privileged-access approval, quarterly access review, and timely offboarding.

Monitoring & Detection

Centralized logging, SIEM monitoring, anomaly investigation, IDS alerting, and documented review responsibilities.

Vulnerability Management

Scheduled scanning, severity-based remediation, patching, configuration hardening, and verification of corrective action.

Resilience & Recovery

Encrypted backups, off-site replication, restoration testing, redundancy planning, UPS, generator support, and recovery accountability.

Internet-Facing Security

DMZ segmentation, deny-by-default firewall rules, TLS, secure remote administration, supported software, and WAF protection.

Policy & Governance

Security policy maintenance, awareness training, clear responsibilities, vendor-risk considerations, and recurring control review.

Professional relevance

Skills that transfer to IT risk, compliance, GRC, and audit-oriented roles.

The case studies demonstrate structured problem solving: identify control gaps, evaluate risk, connect recommendations to business impact, and communicate practical next actions. The work emphasizes controls, governance, documentation, reporting, and risk-informed decision making.
